# SHARING AND FEEDBACK

After joining the **Workforce Bootcamp - First Cloud AI Journey** internship program, I had the opportunity to reflect more clearly on my learning journey. This was not only a period for learning AWS services, but also a process of building technical thinking, self-learning habits, progress tracking, and the discipline to complete work through clear weekly goals.

## 1. Appreciation

First, I would like to express my sincere thanks to the mentors, organizers, and AWS Study Group community for creating a practical, open, and motivating learning environment. Thanks to the clear roadmap, detailed learning materials, and experience-sharing sessions from the community, I was able to gradually build a foundation in cloud computing and understand how to apply that knowledge to real situations.

I especially appreciate how the program encouraged learners to practice independently, record progress, and present outcomes. This helped me learn not only to understand, but also to build, explain, and continuously improve.

## 2. Values I gained

Through the program, I gained many important skills and lessons:

* **AWS Cloud foundation**: I developed a better understanding of core services such as IAM, VPC, EC2, S3, RDS, DynamoDB, Lambda, API Gateway, CloudWatch, and CI/CD.
* **Architecture design ability**: I learned how to read, analyze, and draw cloud architecture diagrams based on system workflows.
* **Hands-on practice**: I practiced deploying labs, verifying results, troubleshooting issues, and documenting implementation steps.
* **Technical writing**: I improved my ability to write worklogs, technical blogs, event reflections, and summary reports.
* **Self-learning mindset**: I became more proactive in reading documentation, following guides, experimenting, and improving week by week.

The most valuable part for me was the habit of learning systematically. Each week had a goal, a plan, a deliverable, and a self-evaluation section. This is an important habit for working in a real technical environment.

## 3. Suggestions for improvement

To make the program even better, I would like to share a few constructive suggestions:

* Add short periodic review sessions where mentors can give direct feedback on worklogs, architecture diagrams, or blog posts.
* Provide a weekly checklist template so learners can easily verify whether they have completed the key requirements.
* Organize more sessions about real AWS usage in companies, such as cost management, account security, system monitoring, and incident response.
* For the capstone project, a clear evaluation rubric from the beginning would help learners better plan their final product.

These suggestions come from my hope that the program will continue to be a high-quality, practical, and meaningful learning journey for future participants.

## 4. Direction after the internship

After completing the program, I would like to continue developing toward a **Cloud Engineer / Solutions Architect** path. I will keep strengthening my AWS knowledge, practicing with more real-world labs, and preparing for the **AWS Certified Solutions Architect - Associate (SAA-C03)** certification.

I also want to maintain the habit of writing technical documentation and sharing knowledge. Writing down what I have learned helps me understand it more deeply, while also giving back to the learning community that has supported me throughout this journey.

## 5. Closing words

This internship program is an important milestone in my learning journey. I know that there are still many areas where I need to improve, but after the program, I feel more confident in my ability to self-study, approach new technologies, and complete a structured technical roadmap.

I sincerely thank the mentors, organizers, and community for accompanying, supporting, and inspiring me throughout the program. This experience will be a valuable foundation for my continued learning, practice, and growth in the cloud computing field.
